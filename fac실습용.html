WORK IN GROUPS. PEER & PAIR PROGRAMMING. I found out about FAC through a Medium article 4 months ago. As I was meaning to take my interest in coding it's very similar to art institution. and that is the environment where I feel most comfortable to be able to perform my best. I see FAC as this playground where people can be comfortable enough to be their true selves, be creative, and act. surrounded by people who share similar values and work towards similar goals, eager to learn From attending the Monday meet-ups I‚Äôve learnt something of the ethos and the supportive peer-led learning environment of Founders and Coders. Because soft skills are as important as writing good code. Whether in promoting for bootcamps or in how-to/know-how advices, becoming a developer is often potrayed as 'grab a skill set in X amount of time and you'll be all set' kind of thing. But the more I listen to developers and look into the nature of this industry, I realise that it's soft skills that makes a developer thrive in a long term. You might be able to teach yourself some programming language, but improving your adaptability, empathy, as well as the ability to navigate sticky situations whilst working with others is something you get to grasp once you are in a professional setting. So I feel FAC's intensive course that emphasises on cooperation will highly benefit beginners like myself in becoming a valuable developer, rather than (becoming) just a person who writes code as they are told.

I want to give back. I generally love sharing my knowledge and experience with other people. When I learn something, I constantly find myself asking, ‚ÄúOk, how would I explain this to someone? I'm excited to take this(my attitude, teaching) further and contribute in shaping the community. it would be a previlage. I'm often in awe of how supportive coding community is. FAC's peer-to-peer learning sparked pure excitement. Besides my love for teaching, I am also passionate about generally love sharing my knowledge and experience with other people.
